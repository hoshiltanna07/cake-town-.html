— The Man Behind The Cart

Bhau's
Story

In 1987, a young man named Bhagwan Patil — lovingly called Bhau by everyone in the chawl — set up a small wooden cart near Dadar station with nothing but a tawa, a pot of hot oil, and his aai's secret batata vada recipe.

Word spread fast. Commuters started missing trains just for one more vada pav. Office-goers, college kids, cops — everyone knew Bhau's spot.

Today, the cart is a proper shop. The recipe is the same. Bhau still shows up at 7am every morning.

1987

The First Cart

A wooden handcart, a kerosene stove, and Aai's recipe. Dadar West, platform side.

1995

The Queue Gets Longer

Featured in Maharashtra Times. A local celebrity is born.

2004

First Proper Shop

Moved from the cart to a small 100 sq ft shop. The tawa remains the same.

2015

The Bhau Special Arrives

A secret menu item that's now more famous than anything on the board.

Today

Still Here. Still ₹20.

5000+ vada pavs a day. Bhau still runs the tawa every single morning.
